# House of Patani content guide

## Product titles

Use the precise customer-facing name. A useful pattern is `[distinctive name] + [verified product type]`. For books, use the full published title and add the author only when verified. Avoid generic titles such as “Book 1” or “Traditional Item”. Do not add a fabric, colour, weave, region, author, edition, or handmade claim unless the product record verifies it.

Examples of structure—not facts to copy:

- Patani: `[verified colour/design] Patani`
- Book: `[full published title] — [verified author]`
- Jewellery: `[distinctive design] [item type]`
- Handicraft: `[distinctive name] [object type]`

## Descriptions

The short description should explain what the product is in one or two specific sentences. The full description can cover, when verified: introduction, cultural relevance, material, colour/design, suitable use, product details, care, and shipping/returns. Keep each product distinct.

Safe fallback when no stronger copy exists: “Discover {Product Name} from House of Patani, selected as part of our collection of Koch Rajbanshi heritage and culturally inspired products.”

Do not use “authentic”, “handwoven”, “artisan-made”, “organic”, certification claims, historical claims, or named origins without evidence in the product record.

## SEO descriptions

An optional `seo_description` would be useful in the future, but no database field was added in Phase 2. Until then, use the strongest short description or the sanitized beginning of the full description. Aim for natural, specific copy of roughly 120–160 characters.

## Images and alt text

Upload stable public images. Main image alt text follows `{Product Name} from House of Patani`; additional images use `{Product Name} — image 2` unless a view such as front, side, or detail is explicitly known. Decorative imagery should have empty alt text.

## Books

Record author, language, subject, ISBN, edition, and publisher only when verified. Book schema could be useful once these fields exist consistently; Product schema remains appropriate for the current commerce records.
